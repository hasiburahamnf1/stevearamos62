A Simple guide to Setting up a limited company

Are you thinking about setting up a limited company and feeling overwhelmed? Being unsure is not new and is the triggering point to know more about the setting up procedures. Here is the procedure for setting up limited company :

Name Approval

The first step is name approval which requires providing 2 names for your company. One name will be required, but ensure the name suggests your business and is unique. It offers an outline of the company’s objective. In case, the proposed name has another existing company in the same then, it is a must to get NOC from that company. In case it has Trademark safeguarded, submit the details.
setting up limited company
Documents required: • 2 passport-size photographs
• Passport and other identity proof
• Submit MOA (Memorandum of Association) and AOA ( Articles of Association)

Other documents drafted include the Incorporation certification offering proof of the existence of the company. A consent letter and interest of disclosure of directors in a draft.

Authorized proposed capital and each share capital subscribed, registered office details, relevant office address, and an undertaking.

Details of companies of the proposed directors hold interest or are already the directors of other companies. A true copy certified of foreign parent company documents with AOA and address proof.

Apply for TAN and PAN. Submitting the Incorporation certificate, AOA, MOA, and PAN to a bank, ensures you can open the current account.

Steps to setting up a limited company

• Apply for the availability of the company name

• Apply for a digital signature certificate (DSC)

• Apply for Director Identification Number ( DIN)

• File for EMOA and EAOA to a private limited company

• Apply for incorporation certification with PAN and TAN

• Open on the company name a current bank account

Apply for DSC & DIN

Apply for DSC and DIN representing the Digital signature and Director Identification Number. An online signature is a digital signature used for Director's PIN and filing. If the directors already have these, jump to the next step.

Registered office proof

Providing the address, identity, and residential address of the directors enables to validation of the company’s registered office address. It should include Proof of evidence and NOC from the Landlord for any utility service portraying the premises address of the owner's name in the document, and it should be within three months.

Unique company name

Use the search tool once you have activated the offer to instantly check if your company name is available.

Registered office address

This will be where official communications are sent, for example, letters from Companies House. The address must be in the UK and you can opt for a Tide ‘virtual office address’.

Director and shareholder information

You’ll need the name, nationality, date of birth, occupation and address of the director. If there are more than one director or shareholders, you can add this detail later directly with Companies House.
